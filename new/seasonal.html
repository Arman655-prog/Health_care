Health and Care Tips

Flu Season

Flu season is here. To stay protected, consider the following:

  • Get winter flu vaccine
  • Who's most at risk from cold weather? Some people are more vulnerable to the effects of cold weather. This includes:
    • People aged 65 and older
    • Babies and children under the age of 5
    • People on a low income (so cannot afford heating)
    • People who have a long-term health condition
    • People with a disability
    • Pregnant women
    • People who have a mental health condition
  • Get advice if you feel unwell
  • Keep warm and get help with heating (should be at least 18Â°C in the rooms that you regularly use).
  • Look in on vulnerable neighbours and relatives.

Summer Dehydration

Under normal conditions, we lose body water daily through various activities. However, dehydration happens when we lose more fluid than we can replace. Conditions like diarrhea, vomiting, overexposure to the sun, and not drinking enough water contribute to dehydration.

Symptoms:

  • Thirst
  • Less-frequent urination
  • Dry skin
  • Fatigue
  • Light-headedness
  • Dizziness
  • Confusion
  • Dry mouth and mucous membranes
  • Increased heart rate and breathing

Prevention:

  • Drink plenty of fluids, especially when working or playing in the sun.
  • Ensure you're taking in more fluid than you're losing.
  • Schedule physical outdoor activities for cooler parts of the day.
  • Drink appropriate sports drinks to maintain electrolyte balance.
  • For infants and young children, solutions such as Pedialyte will help maintain electrolyte balance during illness or heat exposure.

Lifestyle Tips for Maintaining Good Health

1. Maintain a Healthy Weight

Your Body Mass Index (BMI) and waist-to-hip ratio are two measurements that help determine if you have a healthy weight.

2. Eat a Balanced Diet

  • Eat the right amount of calories based on how active you are.
  • Recommended calorie intake: 2,500 calories for men, 2,000 calories for women.
  • Starchy carbohydrates should make up over a third of the food you eat.
  • Eat plenty of fruits and vegetables.
  • Eat more fish, including oily fish.
  • Cut down on saturated fat and sugar.
  • Eat less salt (no more than 6g per day for adults).
  • Avoid getting thirsty and do not skip breakfast.

3. Exercise Regularly

Regular exercise has a host of health benefits, including boosting mood, giving energy, building muscle, and maintaining weight. Aim for 150-300 minutes of moderate-intensity aerobic exercise or 75 minutes of vigorous exercise per week.

4. Get Plenty of Sleep

Ensure you're getting enough sleep to improve overall health and well-being.

5. Quit Smoking and Alcohol (If You Do)

For a healthier lifestyle, quitting smoking and reducing alcohol intake are key steps.

6. Visit a Doctor for Regular Health Check-ups

Adults should visit their doctor for a check-up every 1-2 years if healthy. If you have chronic conditions (e.g., diabetes), follow your doctor's advice for more frequent visits.
